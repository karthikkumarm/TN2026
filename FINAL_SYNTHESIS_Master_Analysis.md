## A.2 — PARTY VERDICTS (3 Sentences Each)

### 🟠 DMK — "The Experienced Spender: Highest Quality; Highest Cost"

DMK fields the MOST SOPHISTICATED manifesto — 8 five-star schemes; zero one-star schemes; and the highest average rating (3.60★) — with its strongest promises concentrated in human capital (childcare centres; school nutrition; Naan Mudhalvan skilling; dialysis expansion; free medicines) and federal structural reform (State List for education/healthcare; 50% tax devolution) that cost ₹0 but could unlock ₹18,000–20,000 Cr/yr in additional revenue for TN. However; DMK's aggregate recurring burden of ~₹84,000 Cr/yr consumes ~41% of own tax revenue and ~79% of capex budget; leaving dangerously little room for infrastructure investment — and the pension reversion (OPS) and 8th CPC implementation together could add ₹18,000–25,000 Cr/yr in long-term liabilities. **The verdict: DMK knows HOW to govern (its schemes show institutional understanding and build on existing delivery infrastructure) but is promising more than even a well-managed state can afford — a rational voter should support DMK's structural reforms unconditionally while demanding that ~₹20,000 Cr of welfare freebies (Illatharasi; gold ring; universal student stipend at current scale) be dropped or means-tested.**

### 🔵 ADMK — "The Fridge Party: Maximum Promises; Minimum Credibility"

ADMK's manifesto is the MOST FISCALLY RECKLESS of all four parties — ₹1,08,000–₹1,48,000 Cr/yr in recurring costs (53–72% of own tax revenue); 4 one-star schemes; zero five-star schemes; the highest EXCLUDE rate (44%); and a gallery of fiscal horrors including ₹35,000 Cr free refrigerators (56% of capex budget in one stroke); ₹40,000 Cr prohibition revenue hole (unfunded); and uninterrupted 3-phase farm power that would double TANGEDCO's already-catastrophic debt of ₹1.25 lakh crore. ADMK's delimitation credibility is 1/10 — the party that claims to oppose Delhi's overreach is simultaneously in NDA alliance with the most centralising national government since Indira Gandhi; EVERY federal demand in Batch 6 is contradicted by this membership; making ADMK's manifesto an exercise in promising what it has no intention or ability to deliver. **The verdict: ADMK's manifesto reads like a party desperately trying to buy back voters with appliance freebies after losing governance credibility; the 3–4 genuinely good schemes (State Agricultural Commission; Amma Canteen revival; tank irrigation) are buried under an avalanche of fiscal impossibilities that would bankrupt the state within 3 years of full implementation.**

### 🟢 TVK — "The Newcomer's Paradox: Fresh Ideas Buried Under Impossible Numbers"

TVK demonstrates GENUINE policy creativity in specific areas — its ₹15,000/yr girl-education cash transfer; ₹25L fisherman insurance; 21-day business approval guarantee; district master plans; and anti-corruption commission are solid ★★★★☆ ideas that show a party thinking about governance reform; not just welfare distribution. But this creativity is fatally undermined by FIVE one-star schemes — the $1.5 trillion economy target (requiring 38% CAGR; mathematically impossible); full crop loan waiver (₹1.5–2L Cr; exceeding TN's entire annual tax revenue); subsidised medicines (a REGRESSION from TN's existing FREE baseline); free LPG cylinders (₹12,000 Cr/yr with zero TN multiplier); and an integrity pledge (gesture; not policy). **The verdict: TVK's manifesto suffers from the "first-time party" problem — mixing genuinely innovative governance ideas with populist impossibilities and campaign branding misplaced as policy; a voter should extract TVK's 5–6 strong institutional reforms while recognising that the party's fiscal arithmetic is in a parallel universe where ₹1.5 trillion economies appear by declaration.**

### 🟡 NTK — "The Fiscal Surprise: Lowest Cost; Highest Structural Ambition"

NTK is the ONLY party whose entire manifesto fits within TN's fiscal reality — ~₹31,000–40,000 Cr/yr recurring burden (15–19% of own tax revenue) leaves ₹33,000–37,000 Cr for capital expenditure; maintaining fiscal headroom WITHIN FRBM limits. NTK fields 5 five-star schemes (water body restoration; 38-district cold storage; Fishermen's Welfare Board; palmyra tree planting; climate adaptation) — all concentrated in ecology; institutional reform; and structural agriculture — with the palmyra planting programme (₹150 Cr/yr) earning the distinction of being the CHEAPEST; MOST MULTI-DIMENSIONAL scheme across all 174 rows and 7 batches. **The verdict: NTK's manifesto is the fiscal mirror-image of ADMK's — low on welfare freebies; high on structural/institutional investment; constrained by one unconstitutional demand (CJ ethnic mandate) and some vague philosophical entries (self-reliance; Annaism-equivalent) but overall representing the approach a Chief Financial Advisor would actually recommend if governance quality mattered more than electoral spectacle; NTK's critical weakness is ZERO governance track record — the best manifesto means nothing if you cannot build the administrative machinery to execute it.**
