Final Recommendation to the Tamil Nadu Voter

No single party's manifesto deserves your unconditional trust. DMK has the highest-quality schemes (8 five-star, zero one-star, 3.60★ average) and the deepest institutional understanding of governance — but it is promising ~₹84,000 Cr/yr that would consume 41% of own tax revenue. ADMK's manifesto is fiscally unserious — ₹1.08–1.48 lakh crore in recurring costs with zero five-star schemes. TVK brings genuine innovation but buries it under mathematically impossible targets. NTK is the fiscal surprise — the only party whose entire manifesto fits within TN's fiscal reality.

The ideal TN government would implement ~52% DMK policy + ~35% NTK policy + selected TVK governance reforms. The total cost of "everything TN actually needs" is ₹27,200 Cr/yr (13.3% of own tax revenue) — NOT the ₹84,000–₹1,48,000 Cr/yr that parties are promising.

5 Promises No Party Made But TN Urgently Needs

Combined cost: ₹14,000 Cr/yr (6.8% of own tax revenue) — less than ADMK's free refrigerator scheme alone.

1. TANGEDCO Debt Restructuring & Tariff Rationalisation

TANGEDCO's ₹1.25 lakh crore debt is TN's single largest fiscal time-bomb. Annual losses: ~₹15,000–₹20,000 Cr. Free farm power (₹6,000–₹8,000 Cr/yr) subsidises consumption at the expense of industrial reliability. No party touches this because tariff hikes = electoral suicide.

Cost: ₹5,000 Cr/yr · 10-Year ROI: ₹2–3 lakh crore · Closest party: None

2. Statutory Tamil Nadu Climate Adaptation Authority

Chennai floods (2015, 2021, 2023) = ₹50,000+ Cr cumulative damage. 85% of Chennai's wetlands destroyed since 1980. 24/38 districts groundwater over-exploited. Without a statutory authority with legal powers, TN faces ₹1–2L Cr in climate damage over the next decade.

Cost: ₹3,000 Cr/yr · 10-Year ROI: ₹50,000–1,00,000 Cr · Closest: NTK

3. Population Ageing Preparedness Mission

TN fertility rate: 1.7 (below 2.1 replacement). By 2036, 20% of TN will be 60+. This creates shrinking workforce, exploding geriatric healthcare demand, and pension burden escalation. DMK's OPS reversion actually worsens the trajectory.

Cost: ₹2,000 Cr/yr · 10-Year ROI: ₹30,000–50,000 Cr · Closest: None

4. Groundwater Regulation & Aquifer Mapping Act

24 of 38 districts over-exploited. Annual extraction: 22 BCM, recharge: 18 BCM — net deficit of 4 BCM/yr. At current rates, 12 districts face zero groundwater by 2035. Every party promises water body restoration (recharge) but none addresses extraction — filling a bathtub with the drain open.

Cost: ₹1,500 Cr/yr · 10-Year ROI: ₹20,000–40,000 Cr · Closest: NTK

5. GIM Investment Conversion Mission (MoU → FDI → Jobs)

GIM 2024 signed ₹6.6 lakh crore in MoUs. Historical conversion rate: 15–25%. Without a dedicated mission for land acquisition, skill pipeline, power reliability, and single-window clearance, investments flee to AP, Gujarat, Karnataka. Each ₹1L Cr = ~2L direct + 5L indirect jobs.

Cost: ₹2,500 Cr/yr · 10-Year ROI: ₹3–5 lakh crore · Closest: TVK + DMK

The Bottom Line

These 5 missing policies addressing TN's existential structural challenges cost ₹14,000 Cr/yr (6.8% of own tax revenue). Combined with the Ideal Manifesto (₹13,200 Cr/yr), the total cost of "everything TN actually needs" = ₹27,200 Cr/yr = 13.3% of own tax revenue. This is well within FRBM limits, leaves capex 100% intact, and would make Tamil Nadu the most comprehensively governed state in India. Instead, voters are being offered solutions costing 3–8× more delivering 50–70% less.
